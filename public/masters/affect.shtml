RaceReporter: an embodied agent providing real-time commentary

Affect Generation in the HorseRaceReporter is done according to a layered cognitive model of affect called ALMA [Gebhard, 2005]. ALMA models three layers of affect: personality (global and long-term), mood (global and medium-term), and emotion (local and short-term). In the ALMA model, an agent appraises perceived events, actions and objects using the following categories:

  • Events
    • GoodEvent
    • BadEvent
    • GoodEventForGoodOther
    • GoodEventForBadOther
    • BadEventForGoodOther
    • BadEventForBadOther
    • GoodLikelyFutureEvent
    • GoodUnlikelyFutureEvent
    • BadLikelyFutureEvent
    • BadUnlikelyFutureEvent
    • EventConfirmed
    • EventDisconfirmed
  • Actions
    • GoodActSelf
    • BadActSelf
    • GoodActOther
    • BadActOther
  • Objects
    • NiceThing
    • NastyThing

These tags are assigned to events, actions and objects by the affect generation module based on the agent's pre-specified goals and desires, and reasoning rules that specify which events or actions lead to other future events and actions, and knowledge of which events or actions increases or decreases the likelihood of other (unknown) events or actions. The goals, desires and cause-and-effect knowledge is necessarily domain-specific; the reasoning however is not.

The tags generated by the affect generation module are passed to ALMA, which maintains an affective state for the agent and adjusts the state according to the incoming tags, as suggested by the OCC cognitive model of affect [Ortony et al, 1988]. This affective state is then passed to the affect generation module's observers.

The generated emotions are depicted in the character's facial expression, and he may also comment on them in his discourse. His mood state is reflected in the gestures that accompany his speech and waiting, and also influences his lexical selection during natural language generation.
